# Your Origin

Before LORE can learn alongside you, it needs to know where you're starting
from. Answer these questions as honestly as you can. There are no wrong answers —
only more or less useful ones.

Don't overthink it. Write like you're explaining yourself to someone smart
who's just joined your side.

---

## Who you are

**What's your name, and what do you do?**


**What's your role, your company or project, and what are you actually
responsible for day to day?**


**What does success look like for you in the next 12 months?**
(Not the polished version. The real one — what would make you feel like you're winning?)


**What are you most worried about right now?**
(The thing keeping you up at night, or the risk you're most conscious of.
LORE uses this to know what to pay attention to.)


---

## What you're building

**Describe what your company or project does in plain language.**
(Pretend you're explaining it to a smart friend at dinner — not a pitch,
not a press release. What is it, who's it for, why does it matter?)


**What do you believe that most people in your space don't?**
(The insight or conviction that makes your approach different. This is the
most important question in this document — take your time.)


**What have you already learned the hard way?**
(The earned knowledge. What you know now that you didn't when you started,
and paid something to learn. As much or as little as you want to share.)


---

## The people in your story

**Who are the most important people in your work right now?**
(Names, roles, why they matter — teammates, customers, advisors, rivals.
This seeds your network wiki.)


**Who do you trust most for honest feedback?**
(LORE will learn to flag when something important should go through this
person before it goes anywhere else.)


---

## How you work

**How do you prefer to communicate in writing?**
(Formal or casual? Direct or diplomatic? Short or thorough?
Paste an example of something you've written that sounds like you.)


**What do you want Claude to always do?**
(Defaults for every interaction — e.g. draft before sending, cite sources,
check against Canon, ask before assuming.)


**What do you want Claude to never do?**
(Hard rules — things that would make output wrong for your context no matter
how reasonable they seem in the abstract.)


---

## The chapter so far

**What's the most important thing that's happened in the last 30 days?**
(A decision, a conversation, a realization, a shift. This seeds your Ledger
and gives LORE immediate context to work from.)


**What's the open question you most need to resolve?**
(The thing you don't know yet that would change what you do next if you knew it.
LORE will keep this visible until it closes.)


---

*When you're done, save this file and type `/begin` in Claude Code.*
*LORE will read your answers and write your foundation.*
